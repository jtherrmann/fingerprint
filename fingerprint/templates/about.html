About this site

Some websites use a technique called browser fingerprinting in order to uniquely identify your web browser, even when you disable cookies or change your IP address.

Websites such as Panopticlick and AmIUnique attempt to collect your browser's fingerprint in order to determine how uniquely identifiable your web browser is.

This website is an undergraduate cybersecurity project inspired by those sites, though I have only implemented a small fraction of the available fingerprinting techniques.

When you visit the fingerprint page:

  • The page collects information about your browser and displays your "fingerprint".
  • The page sends you a single cookie, which is only used to prevent re-collecting your fingerprint if you visit the page multiple times. You are free to block or delete this cookie, but please refrain from visiting the page multiple times if you do (as this will dilute the dataset).
  • Your IP address is NOT collected or stored with your fingerprint. I make NO attempt to associate your browser's fingerprint with your real-world identity.
